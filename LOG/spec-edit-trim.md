# 6fcd9d6 — SPEC.md trim — relocated exhaustive UI detail to batch specs [spec-edit-trim]

This batch acted on the 2026-06-17 SPEC-trim audit. SPEC.md had drifted into exhaustive UI and implementation mechanics — drag pixel-gestures, exact per-drop dates, date-strip dimensions, outliner keystrokes, icon placement — that are build decisions, not product truth. The trim keeps each concept in SPEC and leaves the mechanics in each feature's archived batch spec, which already holds most of them. Twelve SPEC edits landed: the intro was reworded to drop its own "every UI element" mandate (which had licensed the drift); ten feature sections were trimmed to concept-level; the `projectId is null` jargon was replaced with plain wording; and the date-set-moves-the-task rule, previously stated three times, was reduced to one home in §Move between Schedule and Project, with the data-model and edit-dialogue copies turned into cross-references. The completed-task retention decision — tasks are retained, not deleted, when the tray clears at rollover — was folded into SPEC as new product truth.

Findings F3, F4, and F18 were deliberately held back: they live in the Schedule-view and side-menu sections that the parked nav redesign [nav-zoom-spine-and-completed-history] will rewrite, so trimming them now would just be redone. F2's slot thresholds were left intact in the data model (the canonical home for those numbers). F20 took no SPEC edit by design.

The build's second half verified that each relocated detail still lives in its archived batch spec, and backfilled gaps. Confirmed present: F5 in 0004, F6/F7 in 0008, F11/F12 in 0009 and 0010, F13 in 0010, F14 in 0011. Spec 0006 had a real gap — the date-strip dimensions, the capped-linear fade nuance, and the no-date tile's placement and labelling were missing — so those were backfilled, and a placeholder bullet ("initial scroll position per the resolved planning batch") was replaced with the resolved specifics. A judgment call was weighed and recorded: whether to also backfill 0005 (tray) and 0016 (onboarding copy), whose trimmed specifics — the tray's scrollable/append behaviour and the onboarding cards' verbatim copy — are not in their archived specs. Decided against: those were build-time wording the audit did not flag as a gap, and the one load-bearing fact among them (tray retention) now lives in SPEC, which 0005 builds against. Backfilling them would have re-introduced the disposable detail this batch exists to remove.
